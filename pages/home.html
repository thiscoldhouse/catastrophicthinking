Remember the internet circa 2010, when we still thought it was a good idea? Remember when Google's slogan was "Don't Be Evil," and you were glad that Facebook helped you keep up with your extended family?

During the Arab Spring, the consensus was that social media carried a cascade of messages about freedom and democracy across North Africa and the Middle East, and helped raise expectations for the success of political uprising. We were so naive.

The internet was not perfect, but it was hopeful. In the last decade, it has become clear that corporations have not just allowed, but actively encouraged bad faith actors to weoponize the web in order to drive engagement. They have done this at the expense of human rights, global health, and civil society. That website you use to see pictures of your nieces and nephews is directly implicated in ethnic cleansing.

This is a site for a more hopeful internet. The name is taken from Ben Shapiro's 2020 book Catastrophic Thinking, for which he did not think to buy the domain. This website won't track you or display ads. It won't spread anti-Semitic conspiracy theories. It will, however, try to fight those that do - and hopefully have some fun along the way.
